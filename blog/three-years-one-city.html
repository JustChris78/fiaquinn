Three Years, One City: What Manchester Has Done to My Wardrobe

Manchester personal anniversary

As I sit here, reflecting on my time in Manchester, I'm struck by how much this city has changed my relationship with clothes. It's hard to believe it's been three years since I first arrived, wide-eyed and eager to explore this vibrant city. Since then, I've grown and evolved, and my wardrobe has undergone a significant transformation.

One of the things I love about Manchester is the sense of community that pervades every aspect of life here. From the independent coffee shops to the bustling markets, there's a palpable energy that's hard to find anywhere else. And when it comes to fashion, this energy is particularly evident in the city's thrifting scene. I've found some of my favorite pieces in the charity shops and thrift stores that dot the city, from vintage band tees to high-quality coats.

My recent find, a 1990s chenille cardigan from a MIND shop, is a perfect example of the kind of unique, one-of-a-kind pieces that can be found in Manchester's thrift stores. I wore it three times in a week, and I'm still getting compliments on it. It's moments like these that remind me why I love thrifting so much – the thrill of the hunt, the excitement of discovering something new and unexpected.

As I look back on my time in Manchester, I'm also aware of how my style has evolved. I've become more confident, more experimental, and more willing to take risks. I've learned to appreciate the value of quality over quantity, and I've started to focus on buying fewer, better pieces that will stand the test of time. And while I still love to thrift, I've also become more discerning in my purchases, looking for pieces that will truly add something to my wardrobe.

I grew up without much, without fashion at all really, and sometimes I still can't believe I get to do this for fun. Writing about fashion, exploring the city's thrifting scene, and connecting with like-minded people has been an incredible journey. And as my blog continues to grow, albeit slowly but steadily, I'm grateful for the opportunity to share my passion with others.

As I look to the future, I'm excited to see how my style will continue to evolve. I'm hoping to continue to refine my wardrobe, focusing on quality over quantity and investing in pieces that will stand the test of time. And of course, I'll always be on the lookout for new and exciting thrifting adventures, searching for that perfect find that will take my style to the next level. For now, though, I'm just happy to be here, in this vibrant city, surrounded by the people and the clothes that I love.
