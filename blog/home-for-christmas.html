Home for Christmas: What Happens When You Take Your Wardrobe Back to the Farm

Roscommon Christmas personal

As I stepped off the train and onto the platform, the crisp Roscommon air hit me like a ton of bricks. It's always a bit of a shock to the system, leaving behind the hustle and bustle of Manchester for the peace and quiet of the family farm. I've been looking forward to this Christmas break for weeks, and I'm excited to spend some quality time with my family and friends back home.

The contrast between my flat in Manchester and the farm is always striking. Gone are the sounds of traffic and chatter, replaced by the gentle lowing of cows and the rustle of leaves in the wind. It's a different kind of beauty, one that I adore, but it can also be a bit of a challenge for my wardrobe. I mean, where else would I wear a silk blouse and high-waisted trousers to help with the morning chores? Not my brightest idea, I admit, but I just couldn't resist the urge to dress up, even if it was just for the cows.

My mum, Máire, raised an eyebrow as I walked into the kitchen, scarf wrapped elegantly around my neck. "Where would you wear that to?" she asked, amused. I just laughed and told her it was for the benefit of the farm animals, who clearly have a keen eye for fashion. It's moments like these that remind me of the differences between my life in Manchester and my life on the farm. In the city, I can get away with wearing pretty much anything, but here, practicality tends to win out.

After a few days of farm life, I was itching to get into town and do some shopping. I'd heard that Boyle, the nearest town, had a vintage shop that was worth checking out. I was excited to see what treasures I could find, but as it turned out, the pickings were a bit slim. Compared to the vintage shops in Manchester, this one was tiny, with a limited selection of pieces that didn't quite live up to my expectations. Still, I managed to find a few interesting items to add to my collection, and it was nice to support a local business.

As I made my way back to the farm, I couldn't help but think about the different pace of life here. It's slower, more relaxed, and it's a great opportunity for me to unwind and recharge. I'm looking forward to spending the rest of the holiday season with my loved ones, even if it means putting my wardrobe on the back burner for a while.
