September in Manchester: Where to Start If You've Never Thrifted Here

guide beginners Manchester

As the summer draws to a close, and the new academic year begins, I'm excited to welcome a fresh wave of students to Manchester. As someone who's been living in this vibrant city for a while now, I'm always eager to share my knowledge of the local charity shop circuit with newcomers. If you're new to Manchester, and you're looking to get into thrifting, you're in luck – this city is a treasure trove of second-hand delights.

My thrifting journey usually begins in Chorlton, where I live. The MIND shop on Beech Road is a favorite of mine, and I always seem to find something unique and interesting there. From vintage band tees to high-quality coats, this shop has it all. I also love browsing through the Oxfam shop on Wilmslow Road, which always seems to have a great selection of donated items.

Of course, no thrifting adventure in Manchester would be complete without a trip to the Northern Quarter. This vibrant neighborhood is home to some of the city's best charity shops, including the amazing Cancer Research UK shop on Tib Street. I've found some incredible pieces here, from vintage dresses to statement accessories. And if you're feeling adventurous, be sure to check out the nearby Affleck's Palace, which is a haven for vintage clothing and quirky souvenirs.

One of the things I love about thrifting in Manchester is the sense of community that comes with it. I've met so many like-minded people in the charity shops and thrift stores, all of whom share a passion for sustainable fashion and unique, one-of-a-kind pieces. Whether you're a seasoned thrifter or just starting out, you're sure to find a warm welcome in this city's vibrant thrifting scene.

If you're willing to venture a bit further afield, I highly recommend a trip to Bury Market. This bustling marketplace is home to a vast array of stalls selling everything from vintage clothing to antique furniture. I've found some incredible deals here, and it's always a fun day out. Plus, the market is easily accessible by tram, making it a great option for those without cars.

As the new academic year begins, I'm excited to see how the city's thrifting scene will evolve. With so many new students arriving in Manchester, I'm hoping to discover some fresh perspectives and approaches to sustainable fashion. Whether you're a seasoned thrifter or just starting out, I invite you to join me on this journey, and to explore the many wonders of Manchester's charity shop circuit.
