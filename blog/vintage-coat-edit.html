The Vintage Coat Edit: Five Things to Look For Before You Buy

coats vintage guide

There's something special about finding a great vintage coat – it's like discovering a treasure in a thrift store. But it's not always easy to know what to look for, especially if you're new to vintage shopping. Over the years, I've developed a bit of a system, and I thought I'd share it with you.

First and foremost, it's all about the fabric. Natural fibers like wool, cotton, and silk are generally a good sign – they tend to age better and hold up to wear and tear. Synthetic fibers, on the other hand, can be a bit more hit-or-miss. I've found some amazing vintage coats made from synthetic materials, but they can also be prone to pilling or fading.

The lining is another important thing to check. A well-made coat will have a beautiful lining that's just as impressive as the exterior. Look for signs of wear and tear, like fraying or stains, and make sure the lining is still intact. A good lining can tell you a lot about the quality of the coat, and whether it's been well-loved over the years.

Seams are another key factor. Hand-finished seams are a sign of a high-quality coat, while machine-made seams can be a bit more suspect. It's not a hard-and-fast rule, of course – some vintage coats with machine-made seams can still be amazing – but it's something to look out for.

The label is also worth checking. Not only can it tell you the brand and the era, but it can also give you an idea of the coat's provenance. Look for labels that indicate the coat was made in a particular country or region – it can be a fun way to learn more about the history of the piece.

Finally, it's all about the fit. Vintage coats can be a bit tricky to fit, especially if you're looking for something that's intentionally oversized. Look for coats that have a bit of room to spare, but still skim your body in all the right places. And don't be afraid to try things on – it's the best way to get a sense of whether a coat is really working for you.

I'm still on the hunt for the perfect camel coat – it's my holy grail of vintage finds. I've seen a few that have come close, but none that have really hit the mark. Maybe one day I'll find it, and I'll know it's the one as soon as I put it on.
